AI & Mathematics

Timothy Gowers: AI Solving Open Problems in Mathematics

Timothy Gowers shared that he tested an advanced AI model on open problems in mathematics, specifically questions asked by Melvyn Nathanson. According to his thread, the model answered the questions and produced a result that, in his assessment, could reasonably form a chapter of a PhD thesis.

Main signal: AI may be moving from helping with exposition and coding toward contributing directly to mathematical research-level problem solving.

Gowers emphasized that the interaction did not require highly technical prompting. The prompts were closer to:

  • “Could you explore that idea and see whether you can get it to work?”
  • “Could you rewrite that argument as a LaTeX file in the style of a standard mathematical preprint?”
Why this matters: If AI continues to progress at this rate, mathematics departments may soon need to rethink PhD training, research expectations, and how students are prepared for AI-assisted discovery.

This connects directly to broader questions for quantitative genetics and statistical genomics: not only whether AI can summarize or code, but whether it can help generate, test, and formalize new research ideas.

LLM Tip

Prompting Tip: Ask the LLM to Structure the Response as HTML

A useful prompting strategy is to add the following instruction at the end of a query:

“Structure your response as HTML.”

This can make the output easier to view in a browser, convert into a webpage, or adapt into slides, reports, and newsletters.
