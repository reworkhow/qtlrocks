AI + Research

1. ChatGPT is not another undergraduate intern

When using ChatGPT or Codex, the most important thing is not just the tool. The important thing is the person using it: their domain knowledge, logical thinking, and self-directed initiative.

Important analogy: Codex does not feel like another undergraduate intern. A better analogy is that we are asking someone with strong computational training, maybe like a computational physicist, to work with us on computational genomics.

It knows coding, algorithms, debugging, and computation. But it does not automatically know the biological context, the scientific priority, or what result is meaningful for our project.

Domain knowledge

Know the scientific context well enough to decide what matters and what is irrelevant.

Logical thinking

Break down the problem, check assumptions, and notice contradictions in the result.

Self-directed initiative

This is agency, or 主观能动性: the drive to keep asking, testing, revising, and pushing until the work becomes real.

Main signal: AI raises the return on the user's own ability. If we ask vague questions, we get vague answers. If we ask well-defined questions with enough scientific context, AI becomes much more useful.

Why this matters for the lab

AI can help us write code, debug workflows, compare methods, and move faster. But the researcher still has to define the problem, provide the biological context, and judge whether the answer makes sense.

My takeaway: ChatGPT is useful only when the user brings domain knowledge, logical thinking, and self-directed initiative. The model may know coding and computation, but the researcher must define the problem, provide context, evaluate the result, and keep pushing. AI does not remove the need for agency; it rewards people who already have it.
Writing + Revision

2. No empty phrases

Writing note: polished language is useful only when each sentence adds concrete information.

The note above makes a practical writing point: avoid empty phrases. These are phrases that sound polished but do not actually say anything. ChatGPT often writes this way, so we need to be careful that our scientific writing does not become vague and AI-like.

Empty
"metabolomics-proteomics framework"
Problem
What is this concretely? A model, a dataset, a correlation analysis, a causal hypothesis, a pipeline, or a biological mechanism?
Better
Name the exact data, method, comparison, and biological question.
Main signal: Every sentence should add information. If a sentence only sounds good but does not make the argument more concrete, remove it or rewrite it.

A useful test

  • Does this sentence add new information?
  • What concrete information is being communicated?
  • Could a reader explain exactly what this means?
My takeaway: Do not write sentences that only sound good. Write sentences that say something. In scientific writing, concrete information is more important than polished phrasing.
AI Agents + Evaluation

3. Agents' Last Exam: why agents still fail

Agents' Last Exam is a benchmark for evaluating AI agents on real professional workflows. These are not short question-answer tasks. They are long-horizon tasks that require real software use, files, GUI interaction, CLI work, planning, and verifiable deliverables.

The benchmark covers broad professional work: 13 industry clusters, 55 subfields, and more than 1,000 tasks. It is designed to measure whether agents can do economically valuable work, not just score well on toy tasks.

Best overall pass rate
24.0%
Codex with GPT-5.5 on the public benchmark.
Not-full-pass rate
76.0%
The strongest reported setup still fails to fully pass most tasks.
Hardest tier average
<1%
Average full pass rate across mainstream configurations.
Performance summary: Current agents are still far from solving the hardest tasks. The paper reports that the hardest tier remains far from saturated, with the average full pass rate below 1% across mainstream harness and backbone configurations.

Top failure reason

The largest broad failure category is Approach errors at 47%. The largest single subcategory is Wrong Strategy at 30%. Understanding failures are also large at 31%, especially domain knowledge gaps at 25%.

Approach errors
47%
Largest broad category: the agent understood enough to act, but chose the wrong plan or stopped too early.
Wrong Strategy
30%
Largest single subcategory: the agent chose a fundamentally wrong method or violated task constraints.
Understanding failures
31%
The agent lacked knowledge, misunderstood the task, or fabricated information.
Domain knowledge gaps
25%
A domain expert would likely have avoided the mistake.
Why this matters: Agents often do not fail only because they cannot click, type, or run code. They fail because they choose the wrong plan or lack the domain knowledge needed to solve the task correctly.
My takeaway: This connects directly with the first point. For our lab, AI agents are useful for computational work, but they still need strong human guidance. We should define the scientific question, provide domain context, check the plan, and verify the result carefully. The agent can move fast, but the researcher still has to know where it should go.

Reflection + Growth

4. A prompt for understanding yourself

One useful way to use ChatGPT is not only to answer technical questions, but to better understand yourself. Surprisingly, sometimes we know very little about our own preferences, motivation, and working style until someone asks the right question.

Prompt to try with ChatGPT
Do you want to be a small fish or a big fish, in a small tank or a big tank?
My takeaway: This is not about choosing the "best" identity. It is a way to start a conversation with ChatGPT about what kind of environment helps you grow. The useful part is the follow-up: why did you choose that answer, what are you avoiding, and what kind of challenge do you actually want next?
Summary

Key takeaways

  • AI rewards users with domain knowledge, logical thinking, and self-directed initiative.
  • Codex is not just an intern; it is more like a strong computational collaborator that still needs scientific context.
  • Empty phrases make writing sound polished but weak. Every sentence should add concrete information.
  • Agents' Last Exam shows that current agents still fail often on real professional workflows.
  • The biggest failure pattern is not tool use alone; it is wrong strategy and missing domain knowledge.
  • ChatGPT can also be used as a mirror: good prompts can help us understand our own goals and working style.
