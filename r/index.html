Lab Responsibilities

  • Fulfill all university, department, graduate group, and graduate school requirements.
  • Maintain a regular lab presence during core working hours, typically 9 a.m. to 5 p.m., with flexibility for coursework, teaching, research activities, approved remote work, and individual circumstances. Lab members are expected to be available for meetings, collaboration, and project discussions during normal working hours.
  • Provide weekly progress updates through lab reports and one-on-one meetings.
  • Attend lab meetings and contribute actively to discussions, including sharing updates, giving feedback, and engaging with others' work.
  • Apply for TAships, scholarships, fellowships, travel awards, and other funding opportunities in consultation with the PI when appropriate.
  • Plan time off in advance and provide at least one month's notice for vacations whenever possible.
  • Meet agreed-upon deadlines and communicate early if timelines need to change.
  • Practice rigorous, reproducible research by maintaining organized data, code, analyses, documentation, and records.
  • Uphold high standards of research integrity, including accurate reporting, responsible data use, appropriate attribution, and transparent communication about results.
  • Promote collaboration, respect, and open communication. Every lab member's input is valued, and discrimination, exclusion, harassment, or disrespectful behavior will not be tolerated.
